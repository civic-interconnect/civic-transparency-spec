# Survey of Related Efforts and Research Groups

This draft page lists relevant organizations and initiatives whose work overlaps with or informs the goals of Civic Transparency.

## Open Standards & Provenance Technology

- **C2PA (Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity)**  
  A standardization effort led by Adobe, Microsoft, BBC, and others to embed media provenance into digital assets.

- **Starling Lab (Stanford + USC Shoah Foundation)**  
  Applies cryptographic methods to ensure the integrity of digital media and memory.

- **Adobe Content Credentials**  
  Creator-facing implementation of C2PA providing visible provenance and editing history.

## Coordination & Inauthentic Behavior Research

- **Observatory on Social Media (Indiana University)**  
  Maintainers of tools like Botometer and Hoaxy for analyzing bot-like behavior and network diffusion.

- **Graphika**  
  Investigates online narrative ecosystems, often for governments or nonprofits.

- **DFRLab (Digital Forensic Research Lab, Atlantic Council)**  
  Conducts field and open-source investigations of information operations.

- **Global Disinformation Index (GDI)**  
  Provides domain-level risk scores for disinformation, platform-agnostic.

- **Meedan**  
  Develops open-source tools and training programs for media verification and fact-checking.

## Open-Source Civic-Tech Projects

- **Pol.is**  
  A real-time, open-source deliberation platform for participatory governance.

- **SMAT (Social Media Analysis Toolkit)**  
  A civic-tech toolkit from UCLA for exploring social media discourse and coordination.

- **MIT Media Lab: Laboratory for Social Machines**  
  Now archived; previously explored networked public narratives and civic media.
